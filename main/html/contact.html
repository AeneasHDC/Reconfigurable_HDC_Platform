Our Team

MARCO ANGIOLI

Marco is a PhD. student at the Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. He received his M.S. degree (cum laude) in electronics engineering in 2022. His current research activity focuses on the digital design of hardware accelerators for the implementation and execution of artificial intelligence algorithms on low-power embedded systems with stringent real-time requirements.

Email: marco.angioli@uniroma1.it

SAEID JAMILI

Saeid is currently a PhD candidate at Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. He received his M.S. degree in Electronics Engineering with cum laude distinction from the same university in 2022. His current research is primarily focused on the design and implementation of reconfigurable processors for edge intelligence devices and the hardware emulation of digital intellectual properties (IPs).

Email: saeid.jamili@uniroma1.it

MARCELLO BARBIROTTA

Marcello received the M.S. degree "cum laude" in electronics engineering from Sapienza University, Rome, Italy, in 2020. He is a PhD. student at the same university. His current research interests include analysis techniques and models for fault resilience within digital microprocessor architectures, targeting RISC-V cores and microcontrollers.

Email: marcello.barbirotta@uniroma1.it

ABDALLAH CHEIKH

Abdallah is currently a postdoctoral researcher at Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. In 2020 he obtained a PhD degree at the same university, majoring in computer architecture. His research activities include designing, implementing, and verifying microprocessor architectures, vector accelerators, and morphing processors. Cheikh received a B.S. and M.S. in electrical engineering from Rafik Hariri University, Lebanon, in 2014 and 2016, respectively.

Email: abdallah.cheikh@uniroma1.it

ANTONIO MASTRANDREA

Antonio is a research assistant and a research technician at the Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. His current research interests include digital system-on-chip architectures and nano-CMOS circuits oriented toward high-speed computation. Mastrandrea received his M.S. (cum laude) in electronics engineering and a PhD from Sapienza University of Rome in 2010 and 2014, respectively.

Email: antonio.mastrandrea@uniroma1.it

FRANCESCO MENICHELLI

Francesco is currently an assistant professor at Sapienza University of Rome, Italy. His scientific interests focus on low-power digital design and, in particular, system-level and architectural-level techniques for low-power consumption, power modelling, and simulation of digital system platforms. Menichelli received an M.S. in electronic engineering "cum laude" in 2001 and a PhD in electronic engineering from the University of Rome "La Sapienza" in 2005. He has co-authored more than 40 publications in international journals and conference proceedings.

Email: francesco.menichelli@uniroma1.it

ANTONELLO ROSATO

Antonello Rosato (Member, IEEE) was born in 1990. He received the M.Sc. degree (Hons.) in telecommunication engineering and the Ph.D. degree in information and communications technologies from the Sapienza University of Rome, Italy, in 2015 and 2018, respectively. He is currently a Postdoctoral Research Fellow with the Department of Information Engineering, Electronics and Telecommunications, Sapienza University of Rome. His current research interests include machine learning techniques for prediction of complex systems behavior, distributed clustering algorithms, and neural and fuzzy-neural models for environmental monitoring.

Email: antonello.rosato@uniroma1.it

MAURO OLIVIERI

Mauro joined the Sapienza University of Rome, Italy, as an associate professor in 1998. He was a visiting researcher at the Barcelona Supercomputing Center within the European Processor Initiative Project. His research interests include microprocessor core designs and digital nanoscale circuits. He authored more than 100 papers and a textbook in three volumes on digital VLSI design. Olivieri received his M.S. (Laurea) "cum laude", and a PhD in electronics and computer engineering from the University of Genoa, Italy, where he was an assistant professor from 1995 to 1998. He has been a member of the TPC of IEEE DATE and was the General Co-Chair of IEEE/ACM ISLPED'15. He is in the board of evaluators for the European Commission in the KDT Joint Undertaking. He is a senior member of IEEE.

Email: mauro.olivieri@uniroma1.it
